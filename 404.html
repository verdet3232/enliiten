Enliten · Luminz

Welcome to the Enliten UI/UX Walkthrough

Seventeen scenes. One belief: education measured by what you can prove — not seat time.

Why it mattersYou're about to see the loop that acquires, teaches, and retains learners.

Before the tour

But first — terminology.

Three terms carry everything: Programs, Courses, Learning Objectives. Learn them once — every screen follows.

Why it mattersPrecise terms mean a precise data model — the reason credit moves automatically.

Terminology · 1 of 3

Programs

A Program is what a learner earns — a degree, a certificate, or a badge.
Programs are made of Courses.

Why it mattersOne structure spans badge to degree — one build, an entire product ladder.

Diagram: a hierarchy ladder. The Program layer — a Bookkeeping Certificate card — is lit; Course and Learning Objective layers wait below as ghosts.

Terminology · 2 of 3

Courses

Courses are made of Learning Objectives.
Every course carries its Florida SCNS code — Financial Accounting is ACG 2021.

Why it mattersSCNS makes courses legible to colleges — the bridge to transfer credit and degrees.

Diagram: the ladder's Course layer lights — Financial Accounting, coded ACG 2021, and Payroll Fundamentals — now connected upward to the Program.

Terminology · 3 of 3

Learning Objectives

A Learning Objective is one clear sentence about what a learner should be able to do — written by the professor or program designer.
Alignment to Florida B.E.S.T. (CPALMS) is optional.

Programs → Courses → Learning Objectives

Why it mattersThe platform's atoms — where video credit attaches and flows.

Diagram: the full ladder is lit — the Program, two Courses, and eight Learning Objective pills authored by the program designer, such as Apply the accrual basis of accounting and Prepare the four core financial statements.

Supply

Videos & the Watch List

Find a YouTube video — the Enliten Video Collector sends it to your Watch List.

Vetting: transcript → AI classification → quality score → team approval.

Approved videos attach to the Learning Objectives they teach.

Why it mattersOnly approved videos earn — learner-sourced supply at near-zero cost.

Diagram: a video card from the Watch List travels through four cleared review gates — transcript, AI classification, quality score, team approval — and docks onto a Learning Objective pill, carrying its 15.4 Luminz chip the whole way.

The currency

Luminz

Watch. Pass the video's fresh AI quiz — 80% or better.
The video pays its Luminz: 1 Luminz = 1 minute of passed video.
Luminz flow automatically into every course whose Learning Objectives the video covers.

You're already 9% of the way to a credential you never signed up for.

Why it mattersThis is the retention engine.

Diagram: a passed 15.4-minute video pays 15.4 Luminz. The chip rides on the video card from the start; the quiz-passed check only unlocks it, and the value then flows into three program bars at once — Bookkeeping Certificate to 78%, Accounting Certificate to 42%, A.S. Business degree track to 9%.

The concept UI

Meet Pathlight

Pathlight is the learner's home — where Programs, Luminz, and the Watch List become one page with one next action. Seven sections, then your pocket.

Why it mattersEngagement designed in, not bolted on.

Artwork: the full Pathlight dashboard rendered with sample data — top bar, dark hero band, four KPI cards, the Ember panel, Work on next, the Watch List table, the standards chart, and three program cards. The next seven scenes visit one section each.
